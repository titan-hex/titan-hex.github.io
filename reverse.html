x86 code analysis

Reversing

x86 remains the most prevalent platform for malicious software, encompassing Windows, certain macOS versions, Linux, and the UEFI subsystem. We possess the expertise required to analyze binaries across any of these systems.
ARM & MIPS

Reversing

Although ARM and MIPS malware are less common, these platforms are becoming more prevalent due to the rise in mobile malware and the popularity of Apple Silicon, which uses ARM. MIPS binaries often represent malware for embedded systems like routers, security cameras, and network-attached storage. We have the expertise and experience to handle these architectures too.
Android & iOS

Reversing

Technically, both Android and iOS devices operate on ARM-based architecture today. However, analyzing Android APK and iOS IPA file formats often necessitates extra steps and a deeper understanding of additional programming languages and technologies, including custom VM assembers. We are excited to embark on the journey of analyzing these popular mobile platforms.
Documents and macros

Reversing

Macro-enabled documents and exploit files are common starting points for targeted cyberattacks. Whether it's obfuscated Visual Basic for Applications (VBA) scripts or binary shellcode, we have the skills to analyze them. We offer clear insights into malware design, capabilities, and infection indicators, making complex details easy to understand.
