x86 code analysis

Reversing

x86 remains the most prevalent platform for malicious software, encompassing Windows, certain macOS versions, Linux, and the UEFI subsystem. We possess the expertise required to analyze binaries across any of these systems.
ARM & MIPS

Reversing

While ARM and MIPS malware may be less common, these platforms are gaining traction with the increase in mobile malware and the growing popularity of Apple Silicon, which is based on ARM. MIPS binaries often represent malware compiled for embedded systems, such as routers, security cameras, network-attached storage, and more. We are equally equipped and experienced in delving into these architectures as well.
Android & iOS

Reversing

Technically, both Android and iOS devices operate on ARM-based architecture today. However, analyzing Android APK and iOS IPA file formats often necessitates extra steps and a deeper understanding of additional programming languages and technologies, including Java VM and Swift. We are excited to embark on the journey of analyzing these popular mobile platforms.
Documents and macros

Reversing

Macro-enabled documents and documents containing exploits rank as some of the most common initial infection vectors in targeted cyberattacks. Whether dealing with obfuscated Visual Basic for Applications (VBA) scripts or binary shellcode activated by exploit code, we have the expertise to analyze them thoroughly. We provide clear, detailed insights into the design, capabilities, and indicators of active infection of malware, making complex information accessible and understandable.
