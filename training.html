Yara threat hunting

Training

Duration: 2-3 days
Yara stands as the gold standard in signature-based scanning within the industry. Its uses extend far beyond simple file scans, encompassing process memory scanning, traffic detection, and beyond. Supported by a wide range of security products and online platforms, Yara is known for its straightforward syntax. Yet, achieving proficiency in its application can take years. We're eager to pass on our expertise in this area to you. Our training is organized as follows:
  • Introducing Yara engine
  • Your first rules
  • String-based detection
  • Metadata detection
  • Structure and format detection
  • Executable code detection
  • Obfuscation detection
  • Other Yara applications

Apple iOS Forensics

Training

Duration: 1 day
Analyzing Apple's iOS platform poses unique challenges due to its proprietary nature. Yet, it's a frequent target for sophisticated and high-profile cyberattacks. In our training, we cover the basics of iOS security, limitations faced by forensic analysts, and the techniques and tools available for extracting data in a fully consensual forensic analysis. While we discuss several commercial tools to give an overview of the features they offer, our focus is on providing accessible and free methods for conducting investigations. This approach is particularly important for mobile devices, which contain a wealth of personal information. Ensuring you maintain full control over what data is shared with third parties is paramount. Below is a brief outline of what our training includes:
  • iOS security fundamentals
  • Acquiring device backup
  • Generating and analysing sysdiag logs
  • Getting App Privacy reports and crashlogs
  • Live process analysis
  • Network packet capture
  • Traffic decryption
  • iOS malware attack vectors
  • Signature scanners and commercial tools

Exploit Reversing

Training

Exploits are at the forefront of contemporary cyberattacks, frequently presenting in complex forms that only highly technical specialists can decipher. These exploits pose a significant risk to corporate networks serving as kingdom gate keys. We offer our extensive experience in exploit analysis, sharing the insights we've gained from years of examining these sophisticated cyber threats.
  • Exploit analysis techniques
  • Identification and extraction of stages
  • Shellcode analysis: 32/64 x86, ARM64 code
  • Using kernel debugging
  • Debugging and instrumentation on a physical device
  • Identification of the exploit target software versions
  • Effective exploit detection
